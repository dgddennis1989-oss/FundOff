FundOff was built by two people in Ohio who got tired of watching fundraising platforms cave to lawyers. The whole thing took one day. The smart contract has never lied to anyone. Mostly because smart contracts can't lie. Unlike corporations.

The story

In June 2026 a judge issued a court order and GoFundMe took down a campaign that had raised over $454,000 from thousands of donors. A family lost access to money people had freely given them. The platform had no choice but to comply. That's what happens when a corporation sits between donors and recipients.

We watched that happen and got mad. Then we got to work.

"We cannot take down your campaign. We cannot freeze your funds. We cannot comply with a court order to remove your donations. Not because we wouldn't. Because we literally built it so we can't."

The smart contract that runs FundOff is non-upgradeable. Ownership is renounced. There is no admin key. There is no off switch. When someone donates through FundOff the money moves directly from their card to the recipient's account. We never touch it. We built it that way on purpose because we don't trust ourselves with your money either.

What we can and cannot do

Most platforms bury this. We are putting it front and center.

✅
We CAN remove campaigns from our website
If a campaign violates our Terms of Service we remove it from fundoff.org. This is our right as a company.
🚫
We CANNOT freeze donated funds
Once a donation is on the blockchain it belongs to the recipient. No court order changes this.
🚫
We CANNOT reverse transactions
Blockchain transactions are final. We have no mechanism to claw back funds.
🚫
We CANNOT sell your data
We do not store payment information. We do not sell data. We collect the minimum we need to run the platform.
🚫
We CANNOT modify the smart contract
It is non-upgradeable by design. We gave up this ability permanently when we deployed it.

What happens if FundOff shuts down

The smart contract keeps running. It lives on Base blockchain which runs on thousands of computers in dozens of countries simultaneously. If we got hit by a bus tomorrow the contract would still process donations. The code is open source so anyone can fork and redeploy the frontend. We made sure of this on purpose.

We are also a real Wyoming LLC with a real registered agent. If something happens to us there are legal processes to ensure the company continues. Our operating agreement names a successor. We thought about this.
